Colour Palette

Primary Colour

#FFFFFF
Pure White

Pure white is used as our page background for a clean, simple look. It helps maintain clarity and reduce clutter, particularly beneficial for our content-rich service marketplace app. Pure white is also utilized for the main headings and navigation buttons on every page, creating a striking contrast against the dark teal of the navigation and tab bar.

Accent Colours

#357C77
Myrtle Green

This subdued dark teal shade is used in our navigation and tab bar and can be easily associated with our brand. The colour is bold, yet it exudes a soothing, calming tone. By adopting this colour as our primary secondary hue, we seamlessly integrate its symbolism of open communication with our mission to cultivate a friendly, welcoming, and trustworthy environment within our service marketplace.

#FDE9B0
Vanilla

On top of pairing really well with Myrtle Green, this pale green-ish yellow shade adds warmth to our colour palette and a sense of positivity and cheeriness. Due to its eye-catching properties, this colour is incorporated into the action buttons on the app, guiding users to interact intuitively with key features. We've opted for a pale shade to evoke a gentle and calming effect, avoiding connotations of caution often associated with brighter hues.

Visual Examples

We've incorporated a subtle drop shadow effect on all buttons, enhancing their visibility and inviting users to engage. This effect creates a sense of depth, making the buttons appear as if they're at the forefront of the page and ready to be clicked. Additionally, to provide visual feedback when a button is pressed, we've implemented an inner glow effect, simulating the experience of pressing a physical button.

In terms of color, we've opted for a clean and minimalistic aesthetic. Most buttons feature a neutral shade of white, which is gentle on the eyes and maintains a cohesive look across the interface. However, for buttons that serve as primary calls to action, we've utilized the pale-yellow vanilla colour. This color choice draws attention to these key elements without overwhelming the overall design. This yellow shade also serves to highlight selections made by users when there are a multi-select options.

On top of the colours listed in the colour palette, we incorporated other colours in our graphical assets for the app. These colors complement the palette by maintaining a muted tone and conveying a sense of positivity and friendliness. While they may not be part of the official palette, they contribute to the overall visual identity of the app. This allows us to be creative and include variation in design while keeping the brand identity cohesive.

Colour Contrasts

Our body text meets the WCAG AA and AAA accessibility standards, so do our buttons with black text, some on a light grey background, others on a pale yellow background.




The white text on dark teal in our navigation bar and onboarding page title meets the AA accessibility standard for large text and graphics, as defined by WCAG. We stick to this standard by utilizing font sizes of 24pt and 36pt in this area.


Unfortunately, pale yellow on dark teal does not meet any of the WCAG standards. This colour scheme is currently used as the colour of the active button in our tab bar with a font size of 16pt regular. We are exploring alternative approaches to adjust the active button colours to adhere with WCAG standards, while also maintaining cohesion with our existing colour schemes. Another color combination that did not meet the WCAG requirements is the use of grey placeholder text in white input fields.
